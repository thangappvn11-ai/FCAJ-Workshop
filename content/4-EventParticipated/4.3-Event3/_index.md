## EVENT REPORT 3: LEARNING MINDSET, PROMPT ENGINEERING AND AI-READY FRESHER

{{% notice info %}}
📌 **Info:** Event report on learning skills, prompt engineering, fresher mindset in the AI era, and building projects with a professional software engineering approach.
{{% /notice %}}

### Purpose of the Event

- Help students better understand how to build learning motivation in a fast-changing technology environment.
- Provide fundamental knowledge about **Prompt Engineering** and effective communication with AI models.
- Share the thinking, skills, and mindset that a **fresher** needs in the rapidly developing AI era.
- Provide guidance on how to build projects more professionally, not only stopping at writing code that works, but also requiring processes, structure, and clear technical thinking.
- Help interns gain a more practical view of learning, working with AI, and developing themselves in the technology field.

### List of Speakers

- Huynh Hoang Long
- Nguyen Tuan Thinh
- Duy

### Key Highlights

#### Addicted to Learning Like You're Addicted to Social Media

The sharing session by **Huynh Hoang Long** focused on how to enjoy learning more, similar to how people are often drawn into social media or games.

One notable idea was that the difference between **learning** and **social media** lies in the reward mechanism:
- In learning, results usually come later and require a long time to see progress.
- When using social media, users receive almost instant rewards such as likes, new content, short videos, or continuous entertainment.
- Because of these instant rewards, the brain is easily stimulated, making users want to keep scrolling.
- Games also have a similar mechanism by continuously creating missions, rewards, levels, or achievements to keep players engaged.

From there, the speaker suggested that learners can redesign their learning process to make it more motivating:
- Break learning goals into smaller parts.
- Reward themselves after completing a study session or a specific task.
- Turn learning into a process with clearer feedback and rewards.
- Focus on the feeling of progress instead of only looking at the final result.

#### Automated Prompt Engineering: Enhancing LLM Output Quality

The presentation by **Nguyen Tuan Thinh** focused on how to use AI more effectively through **Prompt Engineering**.

**The Art of Communicating with AI**

The speaker emphasized that working with AI is not simply about asking questions, but requires clear communication, context, and specific requirements.

**Why Prompt Engineering Matters**

Common problems include:
- Generic prompts → Generic outputs
- Wasted tokens → Increased costs
- Ambiguous instructions → Inconsistent results
- Poor communication → Lost productivity

**What Goes Into a Great Prompt**

A good prompt includes: Role, Instruction, Context, Input Data, Output Format, Examples, and Constraints/Guidelines.

**Mastering LLM Prompting**
- Be clear & specific
- Use directive language
- Use delimiters
- Describe DOs, not DON'Ts
- Don't ask LLMs to do math
- Allow "I don't know" responses
- Break long inputs

**Token Economics**

Tokens are the text processing units of AI. They do not completely correspond to words. Costs depend on the number of tokens.

**Advanced Techniques:** Chain-of-Thought, Self-Consistency, Tree-of-Thoughts, RAG, Role Prompting.

Supporting tool: **Proptimizer**

#### AI-Ready Freshers Skills and Mindset in the AI Era

The sharing session by **Duy** focused on the necessary skills and mindset:
- Continuous learning
- Understanding the employer's perspective
- Knowing how to ask questions and verify information
- Identifying personal strengths
- Not fully depending on AI

**Don't Just Vibe Code, Build with Proper Software Engineering Standards**
- Not only writing code that works
- Need processes and structure
- Code must be maintainable
- AI is a supporting tool; Humans are the coordinators

Reference: [BMAD-METHOD](https://github.com/bmad-code-org/BMAD-METHOD)

### What Was Learned

| Category | Key Takeaways |
|---|---|
| Learning Mindset | Break down goals, create continuous motivation, reward oneself |
| AI Skills | Write clear prompts, have complete structure, check results, understand tokens |
| Fresher Mindset | Self-learning, adaptability, not depending on AI |
| Software Engineering | Have a process, write maintainable code, apply BMAD |

### Application

- Break down learning goals
- Write better prompts
- Do not copy AI-generated code directly
- Use AI to support structure
- Organize projects better

## EVENT REPORT 4: CONQUERING TECHNOLOGY, BEATING PROCRASTINATION AND SELF-POSITIONING

### 1. Event Overview & Purpose

The event was organized to provide students with a comprehensive perspective on their development journey in the technology industry. Going beyond dry technical topics like Cloud or programming, the event was a harmonious blend of technical skills (Hard Skills) and soft skills/self-management mindset (Soft Skills).

Specifically, the event aimed to:
- **Visualize AWS Cloud learning:** Introducing practical, lively approaches to learning cloud computing instead of pure theory.
- **Summarize real combat experiences:** Sharing authentic, valuable insights from intense Hackathon competitions.
- **Build inner strength foundations:** Clarifying the importance of self-confidence and practical product development mindsets (Case study: Tu Vi Dai Viet).
- **Decode behavioral psychology:** Analyzing the root causes of procrastination and how to establish self-discipline.

### 2. Speakers & Sharing Topics

| Speaker / Team | Sharing Topic |
|---|---|
| Huynh Thai Linh | Upgrade your AWS skills with Cloud Quest and Floci |
| The Baller Team (An Khuong, Quoc Anh, Minh Quan) | HACKATHON: "Ha! A lot", "fun", "debugging", "backache" and "sleep-deprived" |
| Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu | Why do we always need self-confidence? |
| Nghia Tran | Career transition journey & Tu Vi Dai Viet Project |
| Khac Uy Pham | The iceberg of procrastination: When "laziness" is only the tip of fear |

### 3. Detailed Content & Key Highlights

#### Upgrading AWS Skills with Cloud Quest & Floci (Huynh Thai Linh)

Cloud computing is often labeled as difficult to access for beginners. Speaker Huynh Thai Linh brought two breakthrough solutions to optimize this learning process:

- **AWS Cloud Quest:** A 3D role-playing learning game from AWS where learners solve technical problems for citizens in a virtual city to upgrade the town. It integrates theory, video guides, and real hands-on labs.
- **Floci (AWS Local Emulator):** An open-source tool allowing developers to run popular AWS services (S3, Lambda, DynamoDB, IAM, KMS...) locally on their computers for free, avoiding billing shock.

#### Hackathon Journey: Fun, Backache, and Lack of Sleep (The Baller Team)

The presentation from The Baller Team realistically recreated the intense 24h–48h atmosphere at Hackathon competitions. "Hackathon is not just a war of code; it is a war of teamwork, pressure tolerance, and the limits of your back." Key takeaways include focusing on the MVP (Minimum Viable Product), adapting under pressure, and how product pitching decides 50% of the victory.

#### Self-Positioning: Why Do We Always Need Confidence? (Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu)

Speaker Quynh Nhu analyzed self-confidence from a practical viewpoint, removing over-confidence or illusions. Confidence is the key to expressing capabilities during interviews and opening new career opportunities. True confidence is built from: **Preparation + Actual Capacity + Accepting Mistakes**.

#### Career Transition & Case Study "Tu Vi Dai Viet" (Nghia Tran)

An inspiring story from speaker Nghia Tran about transitioning into the tech sector and realizing a unique product idea: Tu Vi Dai Viet (combining classical Eastern wisdom with modern tech). He emphasized that a successful tech product needs deep industry domain knowledge and business models, not just clean code.

#### The Iceberg of Procrastination (Khac Uy Pham)

Speaker Khac Uy Pham dissected the habit of procrastination under behavioral psychology. Procrastination is not simply laziness; it is a brain defense mechanism against the fear of failure or information overload. The solution is using micro-steps to trick the brain and acting immediately.

### 4. Action Plan

To convert knowledge into practical value, participants can adopt the following roadmap:
- **Technical:** Experience AWS Cloud Quest and install Floci locally to run test labs safely.
- **Experience:** Register for at least one Hackathon to practice teamwork under pressure.
- **Personal Growth:** Apply the "5-minute rule" to break the procrastination iceberg and prepare personal projects early to apply for internships.

### 5. Event Reflection

The event was a great success, bringing high practical value. It provided an open, friendly atmosphere, and speakers' real-life lessons served as valuable reference materials for students to shape their career path in technology.
